## Highlights

**Statically awesome collaboration.** [Zach](https://www.zachleat.com/), in [Collaborative Editing as Progressive Enhancement](https://www.11ty.dev/blog/collaborative-editing/), outlines how Build Awesome Pro plans to tackle the challenges of implementing and deploying collaborative editing features for static sites in ways that retain static output, are deployable anywhere, and avoid vendor lock-in.

**Speed up your site.** [Ryan Gittings](https://www.ryangittings.co.uk) provides some performance improvement tips in his post, [Speeding Up Large 11ty Builds on Netlify](https://www.ryangittings.co.uk/blog/11ty-netlify-build-performance/). Some of the tips are specific to Netlify, but it has some goodies worth checking out.

**Why Eleventy.** [Tom MacWright](https://macwright.com) makes the case for why he moved from Jekyll to Eleventy in his post, [Eleventy](https://macwright.com/2026/04/17/eleventy.html). Tom is prioritizing simplicity, longevity, and end-user speed. Those are just a few of the things that make so many of us love Eleventy.

**Debugging with AI.** [Nicholas Clooney](https://blog.nicholas.clooney.io) did some debugging recently, using AI. He recounts his experience, not all of which is good, in [The Limits of AI and Where Humans Shine](https://blog.nicholas.clooney.io/posts/how-clever-is-ai/). I have had a couple of similar experiences, yet I am also finding some good uses for it.
