## Highlights

**OK > F it.** [Simon Cox](https://www.simoncox.com) taught me about the Open Knowledge Format (OKF) this week. He wrote a post describing how to [Publish an OKF bundle with 11ty](https://www.simoncox.com/post/2026-06-17-publishing-an-okf-bundle-with-11ty/). I sifted through it, but haven't given it the deep dive it deserves. There's a lot there, so give it a read.

**Return of the Fury.** [fLaMEd](https://flamedfury.com), i.e., Flamed Fury, returns with a complete rundown on how to [Create A Static Site Using 11ty & Deploy to Neocities (2026 Refresh)](https://flamedfury.com/guides/11ty-homepage-neocities-2026/). If you're new to Eleventy or new to Neocities hosting for Eleventy, this is a great place to start.

**So many words.** [Nathan Bottomley](https://gunsandfrocks.com/), a word counter himself, takes those podcast words...and counts them for us in [Adding a transcript word count to your podcast About page](https://eleventy-plugin-podcaster.com/2026/06/adding-a-word-count-to-your-about-page/).

**Yozo lightbox.** [Shiv J.M.](https://shivjm.blog) makes use of the [Yozo web components library](https://yozo.ooo) to bring a lightbox to the images on his site in [My Images Are Now Pretty Lit](https://shivjm.blog/colophon/my-images-are-now-pretty-lit/). And Yozo was created by one of the Discord server's most helpful members, [vrugtehagel](https://vrugtehagel.nl/), so it's great to see it in action.

**PHP is so random.** [Jens Oliver Meiert](https://meiert.com) shows us [How to Provide a “Random Post” Feature With Eleventy and PHP](https://meiert.com/blog/random-posts-with-eleventy-and-php/).

**Find that Shiv.** [Shiv J.M.](https://shivjm.blog) also brings us [Find That Page](https://shivjm.blog/colophon/find-that-page/) where he replaced what was an ineffective DuckDuckGo search with [Pagefind](https://pagefind.app/), one of the community's favorite search libraries.
